PHOTOGRAPHY

"What I Feel Most Strongly About", Digital Photography, 2020

I feel most strongly about my family. I’ve always been really close with my family and I think I’m lucky to have such loving people in my life. I wanted to record the expressions that my family shows each other and the way they look at one another. We mostly stayed inside the whole weekend so a majority of the pictures are taken inside my parents house, the house I’ve lived in for almost 17 years. The first set of photos was taken when we were all together in the kitchen. The second set was when when our little brother had found a recipe for homemade dough and wanted us to try it. The third set was my mom cutting our older sisters hair. It was a fun weekend and I was able to capture bits and pieces of it.

"Reality and Transformation", Digital Photography, 2020

I wanted to focus on the deconstruction of reality and was inspired by the concept of altered reality. This gave me the idea to focus on liminal space. These spaces are transitional or transformative spaces. Examples of these spaces are stairwells, waiting rooms, hallways, and empty parking garages. These spaces are a means of connection, rather than the destination. When the function of these places is lost, it becomes a liminal space. In this way, by capturing these spaces alone, I wanted to take away the context and destination. I wanted to focus only on the empty parking lots and the stairwells. In this approach, I feel I was able to express an altered or transformed reality.
